Avengers

The Avengers are a superhero team from the Marvel Cinematic Universe, created by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby. Founded by S.H.I.E.L.D. director Nick Fury, the team is composed of "Earth's Mightiest Heroes" who protect the world from various threats.

Fictional Biography

The Avengers Initiative

In 1995, Nick Fury of S.H.I.E.L.D. creates the Avengers Initiative after meeting Carol Danvers, naming the project after her callsign, "Avenger." Years later, Fury recruits individuals like Tony Stark and Steve Rogers, though Stark is initially rejected. The World Security Council's suggestion of Emil Blonsky is vetoed by Stark, leading to Bruce Banner being hired instead.

The First Assembly

In 2012, Loki steals the Tesseract and brainwashes Clint Barton and Dr. Erik Selvig. Nick Fury assembles a team including Tony Stark, Bruce Banner, Steve Rogers, and Natasha Romanoff to retrieve the Tesseract. After initial conflicts and the death of S.H.I.E.L.D. agent Phil Coulson, the heroes unite. They face Loki and his Chitauri army, who invade New York through a wormhole. The team, now calling themselves the Avengers, defeats the invasion when Stark flies a nuclear missile through the wormhole, destroying the Chitauri mothership. Romanoff closes the portal, and they capture Loki.
